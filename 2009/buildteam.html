At Team 375, the build team is the biggest sub-team. For 42 days we work non-stop on building a functional, and hopefully, stable robot. They work down in the lab and typically stay at school the latest out of all the subteams, at least until 7:45 during build season. This is after a long, hard day at school, which is exhausting, tiring, and extremely difficult, but definitely worth it in the end. The excitement of competing in FIRST makes it worth it right there, but the knowledge, experience, and memories the team builds along the way are worth so much more. William Tsimerman is currently captain, and Mr. Seigel is the head mentor.
  
    This year's game, "Lunacy", presents a brand new challenge to the build team. Instead of using our own custom modified wheels, we must use special wheels designed to not have much traction. This adds to the lunacy of Lunacy. We test many different ways and strategies to make the most out of these wheels as to have the biggest advantage over other teams, and are looking forward to seeing what they do to overcome this obstacle also.

    With the work going on down in the lab between strategising, building, welding, cutting, etc., it is extremely important to stress safety and organization. Occasionally we get a little out of order, but the mentors always remind us all the next day. Fortunately, we have not have had anything more than very minor injuries happen in the shop in years.
